Several newsrooms are experimenting with local archives that let readers save research material without relying on a vendor account. The approach is intentionally modest: capture the clean article text, preserve the source URL, and store enough metadata to make the item searchable later.

The newsroom pilots emphasize failure visibility. If a page blocks extraction or only renders through client-side JavaScript, the app reports the problem instead of pretending the capture succeeded.

Editors say the most useful early result is not artificial intelligence, but trustworthy retrieval. Once the full article is stored locally, summarization, translation, and annotation become optional layers on top of a durable copy.
